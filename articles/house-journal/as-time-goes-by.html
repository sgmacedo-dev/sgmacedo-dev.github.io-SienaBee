As Time Goes By

Reflections on beauty, memory and the quiet grace of things that endure.

Approx. 4-minute read

Music of the House
Johnny Mathis — As Time Goes By

This morning, the House awoke to the gentle voice of Johnny Mathis.

Some melodies do not simply accompany a day; they quietly become part of it.

There is a peculiar kindness in songs that have survived generations without raising their voices. They ask for nothing. They prove nothing. They simply remain.

The same may be said of beauty. Not the beauty that clamours for admiration, but the quieter kind— the one that patiently reveals itself to those willing to pay attention.

An old fountain pen. A linen tablecloth carefully mended. A favourite armchair beside a window. A handwritten letter. A melody that still softens a room after decades have passed.

Perhaps elegance has never been about rarity. Perhaps it has always been about permanence.

This House was never meant to become another hurried corner of the internet. Its hope is simpler. May it become a place remembered for its gentleness.

If, one quiet afternoon, you find yourself returning here without quite remembering how you first arrived, then this House has fulfilled its purpose.

Written for the House Journal

Silvana Macedo
Editor, Siena Bee
